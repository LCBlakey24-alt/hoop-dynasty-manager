# Hoop Dynasty Manager — Game Blueprint

This file is the source-of-truth game design brief for Hoop Dynasty Manager.

Future AI builders and contributors should read this before making feature, UI, data, or gameplay changes.

## Vision

Hoop Dynasty Manager is a fictional basketball management game. The player manages a club, not the on-court action directly.

The game should feel like a deep sports management sim where decisions affect results, player development, finances, board confidence, fitness, contracts, recruitment, and long-term legacy.

The final product should feel like a game that runs in a browser, not a static website with tables.

## Fictional World

- League name: British Super Basketball League.
- Short name: BSBL.
- Clubs are fictional.
- Players are fictional.
- Arenas, rivalries, legends, fan cultures, histories and team identities are fictional.
- Avoid real clubs, real players, real league branding and unlicensed assets.

## Core Player Fantasy

The player should feel like they are building a basketball dynasty.

They should regularly ask:

- Who should start?
- Who should get minutes?
- Who is tired or injured?
- Who is developing?
- Who needs a contract?
- Can I afford a signing?
- What does the board expect?
- What is the next tactical decision?
- Am I building a contender or a rebuild?

## Core Loop

1. Read Inbox.
2. Review squad status.
3. Set rotation and tactics.
4. Choose training focus.
5. Simulate fixture or round.
6. Read Match Centre.
7. React to fatigue, injury, morale, development and results.
8. Manage contracts, wages, releases and recruitment.
9. Progress through regular season.
10. Simulate playoffs.
11. Review season summary.
12. Prepare for future offseason and next season systems.

## Current Feature Pillars

### Franchise Mode

The main playable mode.

Current direction:

- Choose a club.
- Manage roster.
- Set tactics.
- Set training.
- Simulate fixtures.
- Track results.
- Follow standings.
- Manage contracts.
- Sign free agents.
- Manage board and finance.
- Simulate playoffs.
- Review season summary.

Future direction:

- Multi-season saves.
- Offseason flow.
- Player ageing and regression.
- Generated rookies or academy intake.
- Staff and facilities.
- Sponsorship and deeper finance.
- Transfers or trades.
- League history.
- Awards and records.

### Inbox

The Inbox is the manager command centre.

It should surface actionable messages about:

- Results.
- Injuries.
- Fatigue.
- Board pressure.
- Finances.
- Contracts.
- Free agents.
- Player development.
- Upcoming fixtures.
- Playoff implications.

Every important inbox message should point the user toward a decision.

### Match Simulation

The simulation should make management decisions matter.

Current and future factors:

- Team quality.
- Tactics.
- Home advantage.
- Minutes.
- Form.
- Morale.
- Fatigue.
- Injuries.
- Player development.
- Eventually possession-level events, shot profiles, turnovers and fouls.

### Roster and Rotation

Roster and rotation are central.

The user should balance:

- Star workload.
- Bench usage.
- Prospect development.
- Fatigue control.
- Injury risk.
- Matchup needs.

Future systems should add closing lineups, rest controls, minutes restrictions and assistant presets.

### Training

Training should feel like weekly preparation.

Current focuses:

- Balanced.
- Offense.
- Defense.
- Conditioning.

Training should affect form, morale, fatigue, development and team style.

### Player Development

Development should reward giving the right players meaningful minutes while protecting them from fatigue and injury.

Future additions should include hidden potential, scouting uncertainty, work ethic, coaching impact, facilities and regression.

### Fitness and Injuries

Fitness should be a persistent pressure layer.

Heavy minutes should create fatigue. Fatigue should affect performance and development. Injuries should affect availability and performance.

### Contracts and Finance

Contracts and finances make squad building meaningful.

Current concepts:

- Annual wages.
- Contract years.
- Renewals.
- Releases.
- Wage budget.
- Transfer pot.
- Board expectations.

Future concepts:

- Player demands.
- Agent negotiations.
- Signing bonuses.
- Release penalties.
- Expiry consequences.
- Budget requests.

### Recruitment

Free agents are the first recruitment system.

Future recruitment should add scouting, hidden information, generated free agents, competing clubs, player demands and academy players.

### Board Confidence

The board is the pressure system.

Board confidence should react to:

- League position.
- Recent results.
- Club reputation.
- Financial health.
- Wage discipline.
- Playoff qualification.
- Player development for smaller clubs.
- Championships.

## Future Modes

### Player Career

Create one player and manage their career.

### Custom League

Build teams, leagues, countries, formats, colours and fictional universes.

### Commissioner Mode

Control league format, expansion, finances and competition rules.

## End Goal

The final game should include:

- Multi-season franchise saves.
- Full league history.
- Player ageing and development.
- Staff and facilities.
- Contracts and recruitment.
- Meaningful tactics.
- Fitness and injury management.
- Board pressure.
- Playoffs and champion history.
- Awards and records.
- Polished game-like UI.
